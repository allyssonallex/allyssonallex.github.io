ABOUT ME


I am Master in Computer Science from the State University of Ceará (UECE) and Bachelor in Information Systems from the Faculdade de Juazeiro do Norte (FJN). Currently, I am professor in the BSc courses of Information Systems and Computer Science at Federal University of Ceará (UFC) - Campus Crateús, doctorate student in Management at State University of Ceará (UECE), head of the Information Systems and Digital Innovation Group (GESID) in UFC and researcher of the Optimization in Software Engineering Group of UECE (GOES.UECE). Overall, my field of study covers the intersection between Information Systems and Digital Innovation by investigating the following subjects and their interdisciplinarity: Blockchain, Cryptoeconomics, Software Engineering, and Digital Transformation (ORCID ID: orcid.org/0000-0003-2108-2335; Scopus ID: 56310893000; ResearcherID: Y-5188-2019).

RESEARCH INTERESTS

Digital Innovation through Blockchain and Cryptoeconomics

Blockchain technology refers to a fully distributed system for cryptographically storing a consistent, immutable, linear event log of transactions between networked actors. Being considered as one of the most promising solutions allowed by Blockchain, Crypteconomics studies the decentralized marketplaces and applications that can be built by combining cryptography with economic incentives towards desired outcomes. Indeed, both Blockchain and Cryptoeconomics have been addressed as a source of disruption by several companies, practitioners and academics. Therefore, we aim to investigate through this research line the market and design for creative business models based on Blockchain and Cryptoassets for enabling digital innovation.

Socio-technical Aspects of Information Systems and Software Engineering

The Information Systems are intrinsically embedded by challenging socio-technical issues. Thus, understanding both technical and human aspects of software engineering is fundamental in order to comprehend how methods and tools are explored, and, further, improve the software development process. Aligned with this perspective, investigating the role of management, organizations, user experience, and how to go through the whole software project life cycle poses as a crucial knowledge for Information Systems practitioners. Hence, our aim with this research is twofold: i) investigate socio-technical aspects of Information Systems and ii) develop novel methods, techniques and solutions that advance the way in which software is built.

Management and Engineering Practices for Digital Transformation

The interest in approaching digital transformation has increased at all levels of education, from university to training programs in incubators, accelerators, government and firms. However, many challenges remain about the procedures in which students acquire maturity for developing their digital solutions. Startups, for example, are designed to create a unique product under extreme uncertainty subject to specific marketing and technological issues. In this sense, we investigate how to support and educate entrepreneurs by aligning real-world experiences with scientific and systematic approaches based on management and engineering practices in order to understand, develop and empower the digital transformation in promising projects.
