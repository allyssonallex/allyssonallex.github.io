ABOUT ME


I completed a doctor's degree (cum laude) in Business Administration and a master's degree in Computer Science, both from State University of Ceará (UECE). I also hold a bachelor's degree in Information Systems from the Centro Universitário de Juazeiro do Norte (UNIJUAZEIRO). Currently, I am an Assistant Professor in the BSc courses of Information Systems and Computer Science at Federal University of Ceará (UFC) - Campus Crateús and head of the Information Systems and Digital Innovation Group (GESID). Overall, my field of study covers the intersection between Information Systems and Digital Innovation by investigating the following subjects and their interdisciplinarity: Software Engineering, Digital Transformation, and Blockchain (ORCID ID: orcid.org/0000-0003-2108-2335; Scopus ID: 56310893000; ResearcherID: Y-5188-2019).

RESEARCH INTERESTS

Digital Innovation through Blockchain and Cryptoeconomics

Blockchain technology refers to a fully distributed system for cryptographically storing a consistent, immutable, linear event log of transactions between networked actors. Being considered as one of the most promising solutions allowed by Blockchain, Crypteconomics studies the decentralized marketplaces and applications that can be built by combining cryptography with economic incentives towards desired outcomes. Indeed, both Blockchain and Cryptoeconomics have been addressed as a source of disruption by several companies, practitioners and academics. Therefore, we aim to investigate through this research line the market and design for creative business models based on Blockchain and Cryptoassets for enabling digital innovation.

Socio-technical and Educational Aspects of Software Engineering and Information Systems

The Information Systems (IS) are intrinsically embedded by challenging socio-technical issues. Additionally, understanding technical, human, and educational aspects of Software Engineering (SE) is fundamental to evaluating how methods and tools are explored and improve software development. Aligned with this perspective, investigating the role of management, organizations, human-computer interaction, and how to go through the whole software project life cycle poses crucial knowledge for software practitioners. Hence, our research aims twofold: i) investigate socio-technical and educational aspects of SE/IS and ii) develop novel methods, techniques, and solutions that advance how software is built and how this knowledge is shared.

Management and Engineering Practices for Digital Transformation

Digital transformation is an organizational change triggered by digital technologies that can impact how organizations operate and deliver value to customers. In other words, digital transformation is linked to strategic shifts in the business model, product development and marketing due to the implementation of digital technologies. However, driven by efficiency and growth requirements, many technical and human challenges remain open about how organizations and students acquire maturity and knowledge for implementing their digital solutions. Grounded in a scientific, systematic, and multidisciplinary perspective, we investigate across management and engineering practices to understand, develop, and empower the digital transformation in promising projects.
